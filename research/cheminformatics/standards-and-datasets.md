# Cheminformatics — Standards and Datasets

## IUPAC Gold Book — cheminformatics

- Title: cheminformatics
- Publisher: IUPAC Gold Book
- DOI: 10.1351/goldbook.11421
- Type: Terminology source
- Reliability: High
- Evidence: IUPAC defines cheminformatics, also called chemoinformatics, as the science of handling, indexing, archiving, searching, and evaluating chemical-structure-specific information used in data mining, information retrieval, information extraction, and machine learning. [1](https://goldbook.iupac.org/terms/view/11421)

## InChI

- Title: InChI, the IUPAC International Chemical Identifier
- Type: Chemical identifier standard
- Reliability: High
- Evidence: The InChI Trust describes InChI as a structure-based chemical identifier developed by IUPAC and the InChI Trust, and as a standard identifier for chemical databases that facilitates information management across chemistry. [2](https://www.inchi-trust.org/about-the-inchi-standard/)

## NIST Chemistry WebBook identifier search

- Title: IUPAC Identifier Search
- Publisher: NIST Chemistry WebBook
- Type: Database search interface
- Reliability: High
- Evidence: NIST states that its IUPAC Identifier Search allows searching species data by IUPAC International Chemical Identifiers, including InChI and InChIKey. [3](https://webbook.nist.gov/chemistry/inchi-ser/)
