# Catalysis — Terminology & Controlled Vocabulary

## CAT-T001

Term: Catalyst

Definition: A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without being consumed in the overall stoichiometric equation and without altering the thermodynamic equilibrium position.

Source: CAT-001

Status: Verified

## CAT-T002

Term: Catalysis

Definition: The action of a catalyst in accelerating a chemical reaction or directing it along a specific mechanistic pathway.

Source: CAT-002

Status: Verified

## CAT-T003

Term: Active Site

Definition: A specific molecular ensemble or surface geometric/electronic structure on a catalyst where substrate binding, chemical transformation, and transition-state stabilization occur.

Source: CAT-001; CAT-002

Status: Verified

## CAT-T004

Term: Turnover Frequency (TOF)

Definition: The number of specific catalytic cycles executed per active site per unit time under defined operating conditions.

Source: CAT-003

Status: Verified

## CAT-T005

Term: Turnover Number (TON)

Definition: The total number of moles of product formed per mole of catalyst (or active site) before total catalyst deactivation occurs.

Source: CAT-004

Status: Verified

## CAT-T006

Term: Selectivity

Definition: The ratio of the amount of desired product formed to the total amount of reactant consumed in a catalytic reaction.

Source: CAT-007

Status: Verified

## CAT-T007

Term: Conversion

Definition: The fraction of initial reactant that is chemically transformed during a catalytic reaction.

Source: CAT-001

Status: Verified

## CAT-T008

Term: Yield

Definition: The ratio of desired product obtained to the theoretical maximum product achievable from the initial limiting reactant; equal to conversion multiplied by selectivity.

Source: CAT-001; CAT-007

Status: Verified

## CAT-T009

Term: Deactivation

Definition: The loss of catalytic activity or selectivity over time due to chemical poisoning, thermal sintering, mechanical attrition, or fouling (coking).

Source: CAT-002

Status: Verified

## CAT-T010

Term: Homogeneous Catalysis

Definition: Catalysis in which the catalyst and reactants reside in a single uniform physical phase (typically liquid solution).

Source: CAT-002

Status: Verified

## CAT-T011

Term: Heterogeneous Catalysis

Definition: Catalysis in which the catalyst exists in a distinct physical phase (typically solid) from the reactants (gas or liquid).

Source: CAT-001; CAT-002

Status: Verified

## CAT-T012

Term: Biocatalysis

Definition: Catalysis mediated by biological macromolecules (enzymes) or whole cells executing specific chemical transformations.

Source: CAT-002

Status: Verified

## CAT-T013

Term: Electrocatalysis

Definition: Catalysis occurring at an electrode surface where charge transfer accelerates electrochemical oxidation or reduction.

Source: CAT-006

Status: Verified

## CAT-T014

Term: Photocatalysis

Definition: Catalysis in which light absorption by a catalyst or photo-sensitiser generates reactive excited states or charge carriers driving chemical transformations.

Source: CAT-005

Status: Verified
