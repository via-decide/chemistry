# Catalysis — Common Misconceptions

## CAT-M001

Misconception: Catalysts shift reaction equilibrium and increase final equilibrium yield.

Correction: Catalysts accelerate both forward and reverse reaction rates equally by lowering the activation energy barrier $\Delta G^{\ddagger}$, but do not alter standard Gibbs free energy $\Delta G^\circ$ or the equilibrium constant $K_{eq}$.

Evidence: CAT-001; CAT-002

Status: Verified

## CAT-M002

Misconception: Catalysts do not participate chemically or form bonds during the reaction.

Correction: Catalysts participate intimately in elementary reaction steps by forming transient chemical bonds, coordination complexes, or surface intermediates with substrates before regenerating in their original state.

Evidence: CAT-001; CAT-002

Status: Verified

## CAT-M003

Misconception: 100% substrate conversion implies 100% product yield.

Correction: High conversion specifies only that reactant has reacted; if parallel or consecutive side reactions occur, product selectivity $S_P$ may be low, yielding undesired byproducts despite complete conversion.

Evidence: CAT-007

Status: Verified

## CAT-M004

Misconception: Every atom on a solid catalyst surface functions equally as an active site.

Correction: Active sites are localized topological or electronic features (edges, corners, vacancies, specific crystal facets) constituting a small fraction of the total physical surface area.

Evidence: CAT-001; CAT-002

Status: Verified
