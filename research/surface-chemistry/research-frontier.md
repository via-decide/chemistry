# Surface Chemistry — Research Frontier

### Claim SUR-C035

Statement: Modern surface-analysis reliability depends increasingly on provenance-complete specimens, explicit acquisition and analysis choices, calibrated reference data, and machine-readable reporting rather than isolated peak labels.

Evidence: SUR-012; SUR-013; SUR-014; SUR-015; SUR-016; SUR-017

Status: Verified

### Claim SUR-C036

Statement: Interface and adsorption models remain active research boundaries when real surfaces are heterogeneous, porous, reconstructing, reacting, or coupled to transport; classic isotherms should therefore be presented as testable models rather than universal mechanisms.

Evidence: SUR-001; SUR-009

Status: Verified

### Claim SUR-C037

Statement: The ISO 12944 coating-lifecycle sources used here have active revision work, so their status and applicable edition must be rechecked before production lock.

Evidence: SUR-018; SUR-019; SUR-020; SUR-021; SUR-022; SUR-023

Status: Verified

## Controlled research directions

- Operando and near-ambient-pressure surface measurements.
- Reproducible XPS fitting, reference data, and uncertainty reporting.
- Adsorption on heterogeneous and flexible porous materials.
- Coupled interfacial reaction and transport.
- Predictive coating degradation and maintenance decisions.

No frontier topic is promotable to a research corner until one question, method, dated evidence object, limitation, and relation to the selected spread are frozen.
