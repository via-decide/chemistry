# Surface Chemistry — Controlled Terminology

| Term | Controlled use | Evidence | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| interface | Boundary between two phases, represented by a declared geometrical dividing surface for thermodynamic bookkeeping | SUR-001; SUR-003 | Not necessarily a zero-thickness physical layer |
| interfacial region | Finite region in which properties depart from their bulk-phase values | SUR-001 | Its thickness is system- and probe-dependent |
| adsorptive | Substance in the fluid phase capable of being adsorbed | SUR-001; SUR-009 | Distinct from the material already adsorbed |
| adsorbate | Material in the adsorbed state | SUR-001; SUR-009 | State and chemical identity must be declared |
| adsorbent | Condensed material at whose interface adsorption occurs | SUR-001; SUR-009 | External and internal pore surfaces may require separate area conventions |
| adsorption | Interfacial enrichment relative to a declared bulk reference caused by surface forces | SUR-001; SUR-002; SUR-003 | Not synonymous with absorption or total sorption |
| absorption | Penetration into the bulk phase or structure | SUR-001; SUR-009 | May coexist with adsorption |
| surface excess | Difference between actual amount and the amount assigned to extrapolated bulk phases up to a chosen dividing surface | SUR-001; SUR-003 | Reference-system quantity, not a direct molecular census |
| surface excess concentration | Surface excess divided by the area of the chosen interface | SUR-001; SUR-004 | State the area and dividing-surface conventions |
| surface excess isotherm | Surface excess as a function of composition or pressure at fixed declared conditions | SUR-005 | Not every fitted curve is a mechanistic model |
| surface coverage | Adsorbed number or amount divided by the filled-monolayer number or amount under a declared convention | SUR-006; SUR-009 | Does not prove equivalent sites or random occupancy |
| chemisorption | Adsorption involving chemical-bond formation between adsorbent and adsorbate | SUR-008 | Bonding and reversibility remain system-specific |
| physisorption | Adsorption governed by intermolecular forces without the bond-formation definition of chemisorption | SUR-001; SUR-009 | Can include multilayers and pore phenomena |
| surface tension | Reversible work per area increase for the declared surface; called interfacial tension for two-phase interfaces | SUR-007 | Solid surface free energy and surface stress require care |
| X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy | Surface chemical analysis by measuring photoelectron energies and intensities under declared excitation and instrument conditions | SUR-012; SUR-015 | Information depth, quantification, and chemical-state assignment are conditional |
| specimen provenance | Recorded origin, processing, handling, storage, preparation, and mounting history relevant to surface analysis | SUR-013; SUR-014 | An undocumented clean-looking specimen is not provenance-controlled |
| protective paint system | Project-specific set of coating layers applied to a substrate for corrosion protection | SUR-018; SUR-022 | The ISO 12944 packet is bounded to its stated substrate and project scope |
| coating degradation | Defects or uniform appearance changes assessed using a declared inspection and designation system | SUR-023 | Visual grade is not a universal service-life prediction |
