# Surface Chemistry — Mathematics

### Claim SUR-C027

Statement: Surface excess and surface excess concentration are defined relative to declared extrapolated bulk phases, a chosen dividing surface, and a matching area basis.

Evidence: SUR-001; SUR-003; SUR-004

Status: Verified

### Claim SUR-C028

Statement: For a reversible area change under declared constraints, surface or interfacial tension is the intensive factor relating work to interfacial-area change.

Evidence: SUR-001; SUR-007

Status: Verified

### Claim SUR-C029

Statement: The Gibbs adsorption relation connects changes in interfacial tension to surface excess concentrations and chemical-potential changes under an equilibrium dividing-surface convention.

Evidence: SUR-001; SUR-003; SUR-004; SUR-007

Status: Verified

### Claim SUR-C030

Statement: The Langmuir isotherm is a bounded site-occupancy model whose parameters and physical interpretation are conditional on its monolayer, site, interaction, equilibrium, and state assumptions.

Evidence: SUR-001; SUR-005; SUR-006; SUR-010

Status: Verified

### Claim SUR-C031

Statement: BET surface-area analysis requires a physically justified fitting range and consistency checks; an arbitrary linear segment or nonphysical monolayer capacity is not sufficient.

Evidence: SUR-009; SUR-011

Status: Verified
