# Surface Chemistry — Research Queue

## P0: S089 interface and transport system

- Select one real solid–gas, solid–liquid, liquid–gas, or liquid–liquid system; do not combine incompatible surface, pore, boundary-layer, and interface geometries.
- Freeze bulk phases, temperature, pressure, composition, surface preparation, roughness or porosity, adsorptive, adsorbate, dividing-surface convention, interfacial area, and surface-excess basis.
- Add an authoritative transport source for the selected film, pore, surface, or reaction–diffusion model and identify every control volume, flux, boundary condition, and coefficient.
- Distinguish measured composition profiles from a schematic interfacial region and from a thermodynamic dividing surface.

## P0: S090 quantitative adsorption example

- Select one open equilibrium dataset with adsorbent identity and pretreatment, adsorptive purity, temperature, pressure or activity basis, equilibrium criterion, blank and buoyancy corrections where relevant, replicates, and uncertainty.
- Pre-register the candidate isotherm model and its physical assumptions; compare residuals and parameter uncertainty rather than choosing by correlation coefficient alone.
- Independently recompute the selected relation from raw or tabulated data and retain units, standard states, fitting range, software, weights, and transformations.
- If BET analysis is selected, apply the current IUPAC consistency criteria and freeze the molecular cross-section and reported area basis.
