# Surface Chemistry — Laboratory

### Claim SUR-C023

Statement: Reliable surface chemical analysis requires recording how a specimen was selected, processed, handled, and stored before analysis, alongside its origin and prior characterization.

Evidence: SUR-013

Status: Verified

### Claim SUR-C024

Statement: Preparation, processing, mounting, cleaning, and in-chamber treatment must be documented because these operations can change the surface being measured.

Evidence: SUR-014

Status: Verified

### Claim SUR-C025

Statement: An XPS workflow begins by deciding whether the technique can answer the analytical question, then records planning, acquisition, interpretation, and reporting choices rather than treating the instrument as an automatic composition reader.

Evidence: SUR-012; SUR-015

Status: Verified

### Claim SUR-C026

Statement: NIST SRD 20 provides critically evaluated photoelectron and Auger line data, chemical shifts, splittings, and citations for interpretation; it does not replace specimen-specific acquisition, calibration, uncertainty analysis, or chemical reasoning.

Evidence: SUR-016; SUR-017

Status: Verified

The S091 equipment cutaway remains blocked on one documented instrument, raw dataset, calibration route, and facility-specific radiation, vacuum, electrical, gas, and chemical-safety controls.
