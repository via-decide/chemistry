These packets establish authoritative evidence coverage for the planned spreads. Packet verification does not promote a spread to final illustration or copy status.

## Packet SUR-SP-S089

Spread: S089, pages 177–178

Visual subject: Bulk phases, chosen dividing surface, finite interfacial region, adsorbent, adsorptive, adsorbate, external surface, pore surface, surface excess, surface coverage, and a named transport boundary.

Authoritative sources: SUR-001; SUR-002; SUR-003; SUR-004; SUR-005; SUR-006; SUR-007; SUR-008; SUR-009

Supported claims: SUR-C001; SUR-C002; SUR-C003; SUR-C004; SUR-C005; SUR-C006; SUR-C007; SUR-C008; SUR-C009; SUR-C010

Primary sources: None frozen — Needs Research only if a specific molecular or transport system is depicted

Review papers: SUR-009 — Verified for gas–solid physisorption terminology and reporting boundaries

Classic papers: None required for the controlled interface vocabulary

Standards and official recommendations: SUR-001; SUR-002; SUR-003; SUR-004; SUR-005; SUR-006; SUR-007; SUR-008 — Verified

Textbooks: None assigned — not required for packet verification

Required visual distinctions: bulk phase versus interfacial region; physical profile versus Gibbs dividing surface; adsorptive versus adsorbate; adsorbent surface versus pore volume; adsorption versus absorption; surface excess versus literal molecule count; geometric versus measured surface area; equilibrium boundary versus transport boundary.

Data requirement: Freeze one real system, surface state, temperature, pressure or composition, area definition, dividing-surface convention, measured profile or adsorption dataset, and transport model before assigning layer thicknesses, flux arrows, concentration gradients, or molecular populations.

Known limitation: The verified terminology establishes the layer-diagram grammar but does not supply a universal interfacial thickness or transport model. S089 remains blocked on a system-specific authoritative transport source.
