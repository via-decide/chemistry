# Surface Chemistry — Common Misconceptions

## Misconception SUR-MC001

Misconception: A surface is always one immutable outer atomic plane.

Correction: Thermodynamic treatments use a chosen dividing surface, while real interfacial regions can have finite thickness, reconstruction, composition gradients, roughness, pores, and adsorbed species. The represented boundary must match the system and probe.

Evidence: SUR-001; SUR-003; SUR-004

Status: Verified

## Misconception SUR-MC002

Misconception: Adsorption and absorption are interchangeable terms.

Correction: Adsorption denotes interfacial enrichment; absorption denotes penetration into the bulk. When the distinction is unresolved, the broader term sorption can be used with the uncertainty stated.

Evidence: SUR-001; SUR-002; SUR-009

Status: Verified

## Misconception SUR-MC003

Misconception: A good Langmuir fit proves a uniform monolayer of identical independent sites.

Correction: The Langmuir equation is a conditional model. Parameter identifiability, residuals, alternative models, coverage range, site heterogeneity, interactions, speciation, and transport must be tested independently.

Evidence: SUR-001; SUR-010

Status: Verified

## Misconception SUR-MC004

Misconception: Any straight segment of a BET plot gives the true surface area.

Correction: BET analysis requires an appropriate adsorptive, temperature, equilibrium dataset, fitting region, monolayer capacity, molecular cross-section, and consistency checks; micropore filling and pore condensation can invalidate an arbitrary linear range.

Evidence: SUR-009; SUR-011

Status: Verified

## Misconception SUR-MC005

Misconception: Higher reported surface area alone guarantees greater adsorption or catalytic performance.

Correction: Reported area depends on the probe and model, while accessibility, pore size, surface composition, site density, adsorbate state, temperature, pressure, transport, and competing species determine system performance.

Evidence: SUR-001; SUR-006; SUR-009

Status: Verified

## Misconception SUR-MC006

Misconception: XPS directly returns exact bulk composition and an unambiguous chemical structure.

Correction: XPS is surface-sensitive and its result depends on sampling depth, specimen history, charging, calibration, acquisition, background and peak models, sensitivity factors, and reference data. Database line positions support interpretation but do not replace a measurement model.

Evidence: SUR-012; SUR-013; SUR-014; SUR-015; SUR-016; SUR-017

Status: Verified

## Misconception SUR-MC007

Misconception: A coating that receives a favourable laboratory or visual grade has a universal service lifetime.

Correction: Environment, substrate, preparation, paint system, execution, inspection method, maintenance specification, and project acceptance criteria form a linked lifecycle. A defect designation or bounded test outcome cannot be converted into a universal lifetime claim.

Evidence: SUR-018; SUR-019; SUR-020; SUR-021; SUR-022; SUR-023

Status: Verified
