# Solubility — Common Misconceptions

## Misconception SOL-MC001

Misconception: Stirring increases equilibrium solubility.

Correction: Stirring can change mass-transfer rate and time to equilibration. By itself it does not redefine the equilibrium composition when temperature, pressure, composition, phase identity, and the particle-curvature regime remain unchanged.

Evidence: SOL-001; SOL-002; SOL-011

Status: Verified

## Misconception SOL-MC002

Misconception: “Insoluble” means exactly zero dissolved material.

Correction: Solubility is quantitative and must be reported with a value, basis, conditions, and detection boundary; qualitative labels cannot replace a measured or evaluated limit.

Evidence: SOL-001; SOL-002; SOL-009

Status: Verified

## Misconception SOL-MC003

Misconception: A solubility product is always the product of molar concentrations.

Correction: The thermodynamic expression uses activities. Concentration products are approximations whose standard states and activity-coefficient assumptions must be declared.

Evidence: SOL-004; SOL-005; SOL-006; SOL-007

Status: Verified

## Misconception SOL-MC004

Misconception: A positive saturation index guarantees immediate precipitation.

Correction: A positive value indicates supersaturation relative to the named phase and data model; it does not establish nucleation rate, induction time, metastable phase selection, or transport behaviour.

Evidence: SOL-010

Status: Verified

## Misconception SOL-MC005

Misconception: Heating always increases the solubility of a solid.

Correction: Temperature dependence is system- and phase-specific. A direction cannot be assigned without appropriate equilibrium data or thermodynamic evidence.

Evidence: SOL-008; SOL-009; SOL-010

Status: Verified

## Misconception SOL-MC006

Misconception: Selecting one solvent and obtaining crystals proves that the desired solid form is controlled.

Correction: Solvent, supersaturation path, seeding, temperature, mixing, and transformation history can affect form and particle attributes; relevant forms must be characterized and controlled when product performance is affected.

Evidence: SOL-012; SOL-013; SOL-014; SOL-015; SOL-016

Status: Verified
