# Solubility — Industrial

### Claim SOL-C020

Statement: Solvent selection and process conditions can change crystallization behaviour and polymorphic outcome, so solvent choice cannot be reduced to maximum equilibrium solubility alone.

Evidence: SOL-015

Status: Verified

### Claim SOL-C021

Statement: In the cited pharmaceutical antisolvent case, concentration measurement and feedback control were used to follow a declared supersaturation setpoint; this is evidence for a controlled process architecture, not a universal recipe.

Evidence: SOL-016

Status: Verified

### Claim SOL-C022

Statement: ICH Q6A directs developers to screen for polymorphism, characterize forms, compare relevant properties, and determine whether specifications or performance controls are needed.

Evidence: SOL-012; SOL-013

Status: Verified

### Claim SOL-C023

Statement: FDA polymorphism guidance limits control attention to forms likely to occur during manufacture or storage and treats crystalline, amorphous, solvate, and hydrate forms as distinct solid-form risks.

Evidence: SOL-014

Status: Verified
