# Solubility — Controlled Terminology

| Term | Controlled use | Evidence | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| solubility | Analytical composition of a saturated solution on a declared basis | SOL-001; SOL-002 | Always state system and conditions |
| dissolution | Mixing of two phases to form one homogeneous solution phase | SOL-003 | Not synonymous with dissolution rate |
| saturated solution | Solution at the solubility boundary for the named component and phase under stated conditions | SOL-001; SOL-011 | Metastable supersaturation is a different state |
| solubility product | Product of relevant ion activities raised to stoichiometric powers for a saturated ionic-solute system | SOL-004 | Do not silently substitute raw concentrations |
| equilibrium constant | Dimensionless activity product for a declared reaction and standard states | SOL-005 | Temperature and reaction convention are part of identity |
| activity | Dimensionless thermodynamic quantity related to chemical potential | SOL-006 | Activity is not concentration |
| activity coefficient | Dimensionless factor relating an activity convention to a composition variable | SOL-007 | Scale and standard state must be declared |
| ion-activity product | Activity product evaluated for the current solution state | SOL-010 | Not necessarily equal to the equilibrium constant |
| saturation index | Base-10 logarithm of the ion-activity product to solubility-product ratio | SOL-010 | Thermodynamic indicator, not kinetic rate |
| polymorph | Distinct crystalline form with a different molecular arrangement or conformation in the lattice | SOL-012; SOL-014 | Pharmaceutical guidance also treats amorphous, solvate, and hydrate risks |
| column-elution method | OECD TG 105 method for lower water-solubility ranges | SOL-011 | Apply only inside the guideline scope |
| flask method | OECD TG 105 equilibrium method for the higher water-solubility range | SOL-011 | Equilibration, phase separation, and analysis remain required |
