# Photochemistry — Common Misconceptions

## Misconception PHO-MC001

Misconception: Any photon whose energy exceeds a bond energy will cause that bond to break.

Correction: A photon must be absorbed into an allowed state of the actual system, and the resulting excited-state population can undergo competing radiative, radiationless, transfer, chemical, or deactivation pathways.

Evidence: PHO-001; PHO-003; PHO-004; PHO-005; PHO-019

Status: Verified

## Misconception PHO-MC002

Misconception: Light is the catalyst in a photocatalytic reaction.

Correction: Light supplies radiation. Under the IUPAC photocatalysis definition, a photocatalyst absorbs radiation and participates in the transformation while catalytic turnover must be established for that substance.

Evidence: PHO-017

Status: Verified

## Misconception PHO-MC003

Misconception: Fluorescence and phosphorescence differ only because one is fast and the other is slow.

Correction: Their controlled definitions depend on the emitting state and transition character; observed lifetime is evidence to interpret, not the sole definition.

Evidence: PHO-001; PHO-007; PHO-008

Status: Verified

## Misconception PHO-MC004

Misconception: Quantum yield can never exceed one.

Correction: Quantum yield is events per absorbed photon. A defined product-forming event can exceed unity when one photochemical initiation produces multiple downstream events, such as in a chain process.

Evidence: PHO-011; PHO-018

Status: Verified

## Misconception PHO-MC005

Misconception: Electrical lamp power or nominal LED wattage is the photon flux absorbed by the reaction.

Correction: Source spectrum, geometry, distance, windows, reflection, scattering, transmission, competing absorption, and reactor path length intervene between electrical input and absorbed photon flux; the relevant boundary must be measured or modelled.

Evidence: PHO-010; PHO-011; PHO-012; PHO-014; PHO-018

Status: Verified

## Misconception PHO-MC006

Misconception: Increasing light intensity always increases photochemical rate in direct proportion.

Correction: Proportionality is conditional. Optical attenuation, saturation, secondary chemistry, heat transfer, mass transfer, and kinetic regime can change the response, so source and reactor variables must be controlled.

Evidence: PHO-012; PHO-018

Status: Verified

## Misconception PHO-MC007

Misconception: A generic Jablonski diagram proves the mechanism and branch ratios for any molecule.

Correction: The diagram is a controlled schematic vocabulary. System-specific state energies, multiplicities, lifetimes, quantum yields, transfer partners, and chemical products require independent evidence.

Evidence: PHO-001; PHO-003; PHO-008; PHO-019

Status: Verified
