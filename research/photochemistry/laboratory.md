# Photochemistry — Laboratory

### Claim PHO-C016

Statement: A reproducible irradiation experiment must report the source spectrum and operating condition, optical geometry, distance, vessel and window material, illuminated area or volume, absorber concentration and optical path, irradiation time, temperature, mixing or flow, atmosphere, sampling, analysis, replicates, and uncertainty as applicable.

Evidence: PHO-012; PHO-018

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C017

Statement: Chemical actinometry determines the number or amount of photons absorbed in a defined reactor space, integrally or per time, through a chemical system with a known response under specified conditions.

Evidence: PHO-012

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C018

Statement: The IUPAC actinometry report compiles gas-, solid-, microheterogeneous-, and liquid-phase actinometers and procedures from ultraviolet to red wavelengths, but each system has a bounded spectral and procedural range.

Evidence: PHO-012

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C019

Statement: Temperature sensing and control, mixing or residence-time characterization, and a dark or thermal control are needed when heating, mass transfer, or nonphotochemical reaction could mimic or modify the measured light response.

Evidence: PHO-018

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C020

Statement: The diarylethene actinometer in PHO-014 was developed for visible-light flow microreactors over 480–620 nm and supports photon-flux and average-path-length determination only within its validated chemistry and geometry.

Evidence: PHO-014

Status: Verified

## Minimum S087 equipment callouts

- Source type, emission spectrum, bandwidth, drive current or power condition, and stability.
- Optics, angle, distance, shielding, interlock, and reactor-window transmission.
- Reactor geometry, irradiated volume, path length, mixing or residence time, pressure, gas boundary, and cooling.
- Actinometer identity, wavelength range, quantum-yield source, conversion window, sampling, calibration, replicates, and uncertainty.
- Reaction measurement, dark control, thermal control, and post-irradiation analysis.

The safety standard must be selected after the source class, wavelength, power, enclosure, and jurisdiction are frozen.
