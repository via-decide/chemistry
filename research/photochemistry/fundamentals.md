# Photochemistry — Fundamentals

### Claim PHO-C003

Statement: An excited state has higher energy than the ground state of the specified system; a useful photochemical state label also declares the electronic character, multiplicity, molecular geometry or environment when material, and preparation or observation basis.

Evidence: PHO-001; PHO-003; PHO-019

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C004

Statement: A photochemical reaction begins with absorption into a state of the system, but absorption can be followed by emission, radiationless conversion, transfer, quenching, dissociation, or other chemical change.

Evidence: PHO-001; PHO-004; PHO-005; PHO-019

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C005

Statement: Radiative transitions exchange the state-energy difference through photons, whereas radiationless transitions occur without photon emission or absorption.

Evidence: PHO-004; PHO-005

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C006

Statement: Internal conversion is a radiationless transition between electronic states of the same multiplicity and is distinct from vibrational relaxation within one electronic state.

Evidence: PHO-001; PHO-006

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C007

Statement: Intersystem crossing is a radiationless transition between electronic states of different multiplicities and must not be drawn without identifying the connected state manifolds.

Evidence: PHO-001; PHO-007

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C008

Statement: A Jablonski diagram is a schematic vocabulary for excited states and transitions; arrow direction, position, width, and colour are not quantitative evidence unless a system-specific scale and source are supplied.

Evidence: PHO-001; PHO-008; PHO-019

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C009

Statement: Electronic excitation can change electron distribution, redox behaviour, geometry, multiplicity, and accessible reaction paths, so an excited molecular entity cannot be represented as its ground-state structure plus generic extra energy.

Evidence: PHO-015; PHO-016; PHO-019

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C010

Statement: Radiation is an input to a photochemical system, not itself a chemical catalyst; photocatalysis requires a light-absorbing substance involved in the transformation and evidence that the catalytic role and turnover boundary are satisfied.

Evidence: PHO-017

Status: Verified
