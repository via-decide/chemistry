# Photochemistry — Research Queue

## P0: S085 state and transition map

- Select one molecule or coordination complex with experimentally supported singlet and triplet state assignments, solvent or matrix, temperature, excitation spectrum, emission spectra, lifetimes, and quantum yields.
- Freeze figure-level evidence for each depicted transition; do not infer a complete kinetic network from a generic Jablonski diagram.
- Declare whether vibrational relaxation, internal conversion, intersystem crossing, fluorescence, phosphorescence, quenching, and chemical reaction are measured, calculated, or schematic.

Source boundary: PHO-001; PHO-003; PHO-004; PHO-005; PHO-006; PHO-007; PHO-008; PHO-019

## P0: S086 quantitative worked system

- Select one monochromatic or spectrally resolved worked example with source spectrum, optical path, absorber identity, absorption data, quantum yield, temperature, geometry, and uncertainty.
- Independently recompute PHO-EQ001–PHO-EQ006 on one consistent number or amount basis and document every unit conversion.
- If an atmospheric photolysis example is used, freeze the NASA JPL species, reaction channel, temperature, wavelength grid, dataset version, actinic field, and integration method.

Source boundary: PHO-009; PHO-010; PHO-011; PHO-013

## P0: S087 reactor and actinometry setup

- Choose one batch or flow reactor; do not merge incompatible lamp, vessel, flow, mixing, cooling, sampling, and actinometry arrangements.
- Select an actinometer whose wavelength range, optical density, solvent, temperature, conversion window, and analytical procedure match the chosen source and reactor.
- Add source-specific shielding, interlock, thermal-control, oxygen or gas-handling, and pressure evidence after the hardware and jurisdiction are fixed.
- Report source emission, received and absorbed photon fluxes, irradiated volume, path length, distance, vessel material, temperature history, mixing or residence-time distribution, replicates, and uncertainty.

Source boundary: PHO-010; PHO-011; PHO-012; PHO-014; PHO-018

## P0: S088 mechanism-specific reaction pathway

- Choose one open, non-proprietary reaction with a primary mechanistic paper and supporting spectroscopic, electrochemical, quenching, labelling, kinetic, or computational evidence.
- Define the absorbing species, excited state, reductive or oxidative quenching branch, energy-transfer or electron-transfer step, bond-forming steps, catalyst-regeneration step, deactivation routes, and mass and charge balance.
- Treat PHO-015, PHO-016, PHO-020, and PHO-021 as reviews or context only; they do not authorize a new composite mechanism.
- Freeze one pathway and mark disputed or unobserved intermediates explicitly before commissioning reaction arrows.

Source boundary: PHO-001; PHO-015; PHO-016; PHO-017; PHO-020; PHO-021

## P1: Publication controls

- Keep all destinations in `references.md`; topic records use only canonical evidence IDs.
- Recheck IUPAC Gold Book version, NASA JPL evaluation and errata, publisher metadata, and standard applicability at final-copy freeze.
- Assign a classic paper only when it directly supports a planned historical or mechanistic claim; do not use reputation as a substitute for claim-level evidence.
- Add a textbook only if it supplies a necessary derivation or pedagogical bridge not already covered by an authoritative source.
- Commission no final art until source locators, state labels, equations, spectral conditions, and mechanism boundaries pass scientific review.
