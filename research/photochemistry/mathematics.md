# Photochemistry — Mathematics

### Claim PHO-C021

Statement: Photon energy is proportional to frequency and inversely proportional to vacuum wavelength, but this relation alone does not determine whether a molecular system absorbs the photon.

Evidence: PHO-009

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C022

Statement: The Beer–Lambert relation connects decadic absorbance to path length and absorber concentration only under its optical and chemical assumptions.

Evidence: PHO-010; PHO-018

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C023

Statement: For a defined path in which attenuation is attributed to absorption, the absorbed photon fraction at one wavelength is `1 - 10^{-A(\lambda)}`; scattering, reflection, windows, geometry, and competing absorbers require separate treatment.

Evidence: PHO-010; PHO-011

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C024

Statement: Quantum yield is the number or amount of a defined event divided by the number or amount of photons absorbed by the system, with wavelength and event basis declared.

Evidence: PHO-011

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C025

Statement: A spectral first-order photolysis frequency combines actinic photon flux density, absorption cross section, and channel-specific quantum yield over wavelength on a compatible spectral and temperature basis.

Evidence: PHO-013

Status: Verified

### Claim PHO-C026

Statement: A quantum yield is not automatically a probability bounded by unity; its numerical interpretation depends on the defined event, and chain amplification or secondary chemistry can produce more than one counted event per absorbed photon.

Evidence: PHO-011; PHO-018

Status: Verified
