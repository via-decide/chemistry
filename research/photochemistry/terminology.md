# Photochemistry — Controlled Terminology

| Term | Controlled use | Evidence | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| photochemistry | Branch of chemistry concerned with the chemical effects of light | PHO-001; PHO-002 | Do not label every light-assisted observation a photochemical mechanism |
| photon | Quantum of electromagnetic radiation with energy related to frequency | PHO-009 | Photon energy alone does not prove absorption |
| ground state | Lowest-energy state for the specified system under the declared constraints | PHO-001; PHO-003 | State identity includes molecular structure and environment |
| excited state | State of a system at higher energy than its ground state | PHO-001; PHO-003 | Do not collapse electronic, vibrational, and spin labels |
| radiative transition | Transition whose energy difference is emitted or absorbed as photons | PHO-004 | Not synonymous with every excited-state transition |
| radiationless transition | Transition between states without photon emission or absorption | PHO-005 | Energy can be redistributed into other molecular or environmental degrees of freedom |
| internal conversion | Radiationless transition between electronic states of the same multiplicity | PHO-001; PHO-006 | Distinct from intersystem crossing |
| intersystem crossing | Radiationless transition between electronic states of different multiplicities | PHO-001; PHO-007 | A state label and multiplicity basis are required |
| Jablonski diagram | Schematic state-and-transition diagram for photophysical and photochemical processes | PHO-001; PHO-008 | Not a quantitative energy surface, kinetic trace, or universal pathway |
| absorbance | Decadic logarithmic measure relating incident and transmitted spectral radiant power | PHO-010 | Attenuation by reflection or scattering is not automatically absorption |
| quantum yield | Number or amount of defined events per photon absorbed by the system | PHO-001; PHO-011 | State event, wavelength, basis, and measurement method |
| chemical actinometer | Chemical system used to determine photons absorbed in a defined reactor space, integrally or per time | PHO-012 | Valid only in its calibrated spectral and procedural range |
| photon flux | Number or amount of photons crossing or received by a declared boundary per time | PHO-001; PHO-012 | Incident, received, and absorbed fluxes are different quantities |
| photocatalysis | Reaction initiation or rate change under radiation in the presence of a light-absorbing photocatalyst involved in transformation of reaction partners | PHO-017 | Distinguish photocatalyst turnover from stoichiometric photoactive reagent use |
| electron transfer | Transfer process whose donor, acceptor, charge states, and evidence are identified | PHO-001; PHO-015; PHO-020 | Quenching or product formation alone does not identify the full sequence |
| energy transfer | Excitation-energy transfer between a donor and acceptor under a declared mechanism | PHO-001; PHO-020 | Do not substitute for electron transfer without evidence |
| photolysis frequency | First-order spectral rate coefficient for a specified photodissociation channel and actinic field | PHO-013 | Not a universal batch-reactor rate constant |
