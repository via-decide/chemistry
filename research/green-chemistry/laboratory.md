# Green Chemistry — Laboratory Research

## Laboratory practices

- Solvent minimization.
- Safer solvent substitution.
- Catalytic rather than stoichiometric reagents.
- Waste prevention.
- Real-time monitoring.
- Energy-efficient reaction conditions.
- Reduced derivatization.
- Design for safer products and processes.

## Source anchors

- Source: ACS
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: ACS lists safer solvents and auxiliaries, catalysis, real-time analysis for pollution prevention, and inherently safer chemistry for accident prevention among the 12 principles. [7](https://www.acs.org/green-chemistry-sustainability/principles/12-principles-of-green-chemistry.html)

- Source: Yale Center for Green Chemistry & Green Engineering
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Yale states that auxiliary substances such as solvents and separation agents should be made unnecessary wherever possible and innocuous when used. [8](https://greenchemistry.yale.edu/about/principles-green-chemistry)
