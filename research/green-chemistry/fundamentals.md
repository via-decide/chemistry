# Green Chemistry — Fundamentals Research

## Core concepts

- Source: ACS
  Publication: 12 Principles of Green Chemistry
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: ACS lists the 12 principles as prevention, atom economy, less hazardous chemical syntheses, designing safer chemicals, safer solvents and auxiliaries, design for energy efficiency, renewable feedstocks, reduce derivatives, catalysis, design for degradation, real-time analysis for pollution prevention, and inherently safer chemistry for accident prevention. [7](https://www.acs.org/green-chemistry-sustainability/principles/12-principles-of-green-chemistry.html)

- Source: Yale Center for Green Chemistry & Green Engineering
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Yale states that catalytic reagents, as selective as possible, are superior to stoichiometric reagents. [8](https://greenchemistry.yale.edu/about/principles-green-chemistry)
