# Chromatography — Terminology & Controlled Vocabulary

## CHR-T001

Term: Chromatography

Definition: A physical method of separation in which components to be separated are distributed between two phases, one of which is stationary while the other moves in a definite direction.

Source: CHR-001; CHR-002

Status: Verified

## CHR-T002

Term: Stationary Phase

Definition: The non-mobile phase immobilized inside a column or on a solid surface that selectively retains analyte molecules via adsorption, partition, ion exchange, or size exclusion.

Source: CHR-001; CHR-002

Status: Verified

## CHR-T003

Term: Mobile Phase

Definition: The fluid (gas, liquid, or supercritical fluid) that percolates through or along the stationary phase bed, carrying analyte components through the column.

Source: CHR-001; CHR-002

Status: Verified

## CHR-T004

Term: Retention Time (t_R)

Definition: The elapsed time between sample injection and the appearance of the analyte peak maximum at the detector.

Source: CHR-002; CHR-003

Status: Verified

## CHR-T005

Term: Dead Time (t_0)

Definition: The retention time of an unretained species that travels through the column volume without thermodynamic interaction with the stationary phase.

Source: CHR-002; CHR-003

Status: Verified

## CHR-T006

Term: Retention Factor (k)

Definition: The ratio of the amount of analyte in the stationary phase to the amount in the mobile phase at equilibrium, calculated as $k = (t_R - t_0) / t_0$.

Source: CHR-002; CHR-003

Status: Verified

## CHR-T007

Term: Separation Selectivity (Alpha)

Definition: The ratio of retention factors of two adjacent peaks ($alpha = k_2 / k_1$), reflecting relative thermodynamic affinity for the stationary phase.

Source: CHR-002; CHR-004

Status: Verified

## CHR-T008

Term: Resolution (R_s)

Definition: The degree of physical separation between two adjacent chromatographic peaks, defined as twice the distance between peak maxima divided by the sum of their baseline widths.

Source: CHR-002; CHR-004

Status: Verified

## CHR-T009

Term: Theoretical Plate (N)

Definition: An imaginary column section in which analyte distribution equilibrium between stationary and mobile phases is established; higher $N$ denotes lower band broadening.

Source: CHR-002; CHR-005

Status: Verified

## CHR-T010

Term: Gas Chromatography (GC)

Definition: A chromatographic technique in which the mobile phase is a carrier gas (e.g., He, $H_2$, $N_2$) and the stationary phase is a high-boiling liquid or solid inside a capillary column.

Source: CHR-002; CHR-007

Status: Verified

## CHR-T011

Term: High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC)

Definition: A liquid chromatographic technique utilizing small particle stationary phases ($1.7 - 5 \; \mu\text{m}$) operated under high pressure ($100 - 1200 \text{ bar}$) to achieve rapid, high-resolution separation.

Source: CHR-002; CHR-006

Status: Verified

## CHR-T012

Term: Ion Chromatography (IC)

Definition: A liquid chromatographic method that separates ionic analytes (anions, cations) based on their reversible electrostatic interaction with charged ion-exchange resin stationary phases.

Source: CHR-002

Status: Verified

## CHR-T013

Term: Size-Exclusion Chromatography (SEC / GPC)

Definition: A non-adsorptive chromatographic mode that separates macromolecules according to their hydrodynamic volume as they navigate porous stationary phase beads.

Source: CHR-002

Status: Verified

## CHR-T014

Term: Van Deemter Equation

Definition: An empirical equation ($H = A + B/u + C\cdot u$) describing how plate height $H$ varies with mobile phase linear velocity $u$ due to eddy diffusion, axial diffusion, and mass transfer kinetics.

Source: CHR-002; CHR-005

Status: Verified
