# Chromatography — Standards

## IUPAC Gold Book — chromatography

- Title: chromatography
- Publisher: IUPAC Gold Book
- DOI: 10.1351/goldbook.C01075
- Type: Terminology source
- Reliability: High
- Evidence: IUPAC defines chromatography as a physical method of separation in which components are distributed between two phases, one stationary and one mobile moving in a definite direction. [9](https://goldbook.iupac.org/terms/view/C01075)
- Evidence: IUPAC lists related forms including adsorption chromatography, affinity chromatography, column chromatography, gas chromatography, ion-exchange chromatography, liquid chromatography, reversed-phase chromatography, supercritical fluid chromatography, and two-dimensional chromatography. [9](https://goldbook.iupac.org/terms/view/C01075)
- Evidence: IUPAC cites *Nomenclature for chromatography (IUPAC Recommendations 1993)* as a source for the chromatography entry. [9](https://goldbook.iupac.org/terms/view/C01075)
