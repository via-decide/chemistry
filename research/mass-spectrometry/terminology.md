# Mass Spectrometry — Terminology & Controlled Vocabulary

## MS-T001

Term: Mass Spectrometry

Definition: An analytical technique that transforms molecules into gas-phase ions, separates them according to their mass-to-charge ratio ($m/z$), and measures their relative abundance.

Source: MS-001; MS-002

Status: Verified

## MS-T002

Term: Mass-to-Charge Ratio (m/z)

Definition: A dimensionless quantity formed by dividing the mass of an ion in unified atomic mass units by its charge number.

Source: MS-002; MS-003

Status: Verified

## MS-T003

Term: Molecular Ion

Definition: An ion formed by the removal or addition of one or more electrons from a molecular entity without chemical bond cleavage, yielding $[M]^{+\bullet}$ or $[M]^{-\bullet}$.

Source: MS-002

Status: Verified

## MS-T004

Term: Protonated Molecule

Definition: An ion formed by the addition of a proton to a neutral molecule, represented as $[M + H]^+$.

Source: MS-002

Status: Verified

## MS-T005

Term: Resolving Power (R)

Definition: The capability of a mass spectrometer to separate two ions differing by a small mass difference $\Delta m$, calculated as $R = m / \Delta m$.

Source: MS-002; MS-004

Status: Verified

## MS-T006

Term: Base Peak

Definition: The most intense peak in a mass spectrum, designated as 100% relative abundance for spectrum normalization.

Source: MS-002

Status: Verified

## MS-T007

Term: Electron Ionization (EI)

Definition: An ionization technique in which high-energy electrons (typically 70 eV) interact with gas-phase neutral molecules to induce extensive ionization and reproducible fragmentation.

Source: MS-002

Status: Verified

## MS-T008

Term: Electrospray Ionization (ESI)

Definition: A soft ionization technique that applies a high electric potential to a liquid solution stream to generate charged droplets, leading to desolvated gas-phase multiply-charged ions.

Source: MS-002; MS-005

Status: Verified

## MS-T009

Term: Matrix-Assisted Laser Desorption/Ionization (MALDI)

Definition: A soft ionization technique in which analyte molecules embedded in an excess of UV/IR-absorbing matrix crystalline solid are co-desorbed and ionized by laser pulses.

Source: MS-002; MS-005

Status: Verified

## MS-T010

Term: Quadrupole Mass Filter

Definition: A mass analyzer consisting of four parallel cylindrical or hyperbolic metal rods that uses combined radiofrequency (RF) and direct current (DC) fields to transmit a selected $m/z$ window.

Source: MS-002

Status: Verified

## MS-T011

Term: Time-of-Flight (TOF) Analyzer

Definition: A mass analyzer that measures the time required for accelerated ions to drift through a field-free vacuum tube to reach the detector.

Source: MS-002

Status: Verified

## MS-T012

Term: Collision-Induced Dissociation (CID)

Definition: A tandem mass spectrometry ($MS/MS$) process in which precursor ions collide with neutral gas atoms (e.g., He, Ar, $N_2$), converting translational energy into vibrational excitation to induce structural fragmentation.

Source: MS-002

Status: Verified

## MS-T013

Term: Monoisotopic Mass

Definition: The exact mass of a molecule calculated using the precise mass of the most abundant naturally occurring isotope of each constituent element.

Source: MS-002; MS-006

Status: Verified

## MS-T014

Term: Mass Defect

Definition: The difference between the exact monoisotopic mass of a molecule or ion and its nearest integer nominal mass number.

Source: MS-002; MS-006

Status: Verified
