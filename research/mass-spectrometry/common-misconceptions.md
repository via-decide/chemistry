# Mass Spectrometry — Common Misconceptions

## MS-M001

Misconception: Mass spectrometry measures neutral molecular mass directly.

Correction: Mass spectrometers detect gas-phase ions and measure $m/z$. For multiply-charged species ($[M+zH]^{z+}$) or adducts ($[M+Na]^+$, $[M+HCOO]^-$), the observed $m/z$ peak does not equal the neutral molecular weight without applying charge-state and adduct deconvolutions.

Evidence: MS-001; MS-002; MS-003

Status: Verified

## MS-M002

Misconception: Relative peak height in a raw mass spectrum equals relative analyte molar concentration.

Correction: Ionization efficiency varies by orders of magnitude across chemical classes, matrices, and source parameters. Quantitative MS requires matrix-matched calibration or stable-isotope labeled internal standards.

Evidence: MS-002; MS-005

Status: Verified

## MS-M003

Misconception: Soft ionization techniques like ESI or MALDI produce zero fragmentation or adduct artifacts.

Correction: While soft ionization minimizes extensive thermal fragmentation compared to 70 eV EI, in-source CID, thermal degradation, gas-phase dimer formation, and alkali metal adducts routinely occur.

Evidence: MS-002; MS-005

Status: Verified

## MS-M004

Misconception: Nominal unit-mass resolution is sufficient to determine chemical formula.

Correction: Multiple distinct elemental compositions share identical nominal mass (e.g., $CO$, $N_2$, and $C_2H_4$ all have nominal mass 28 Da). High-resolution exact mass ($<5 \text{ ppm}$ error) is required to resolve isotopic fine structure and assign molecular formulae.

Evidence: MS-002; MS-004; MS-006

Status: Verified
