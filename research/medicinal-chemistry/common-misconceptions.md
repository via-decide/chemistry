# Medicinal Chemistry — Common Misconceptions

## MED-M001

Misconception: Optimizing target binding potency ($IC_{50}$) alone guarantees clinical efficacy.

Correction: Drug discovery requires multiparameter optimization. A nanomolar inhibitor with poor solubility, high clearance, low cell permeability, or severe hERG toxicity will fail in preclinical development.

Evidence: MED-001; MED-004; MED-005

Status: Verified

## MED-M002

Misconception: $IC_{50}$ values measured across different labs or assay substrate concentrations can be directly compared.

Correction: $IC_{50}$ depends on substrate concentration $[S]$ and Michaelis constant $K_m$. True intrinsic affinity must be reported as $K_i$ via the Cheng–Prusoff equation.

Evidence: MED-001; MED-005

Status: Verified

## MED-M003

Misconception: Maximizing lipophilicity ($log P$) is the best way to improve cell membrane permeability.

Correction: Excessive lipophilicity ($log P > 5$) leads to poor aqueous solubility, high non-specific plasma protein binding, rapid metabolic clearance by CYP enzymes, and off-target toxicity.

Evidence: MED-001; MED-004; MED-005

Status: Verified

## MED-M004

Misconception: Discovery medicinal chemistry synthetic routes can be directly transferred to commercial API manufacturing.

Correction: Discovery routes prioritize rapid analog synthesis using expensive/hazardous reagents. Process development requires scalable, safe, high-yield, green chemistry routes with strict ICH Q3A impurity controls ($< 0.10%$).

Evidence: MED-004; MED-006

Status: Verified
