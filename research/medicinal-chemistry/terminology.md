# Medicinal Chemistry — Terminology & Controlled Vocabulary

## MED-T001

Term: Medicinal Chemistry

Definition: A chemistry-based discipline involving the discovery, design, synthesis, structural optimization, and mechanistic evaluation of biologically active compounds for therapeutic use.

Source: MED-001

Status: Verified

## MED-T002

Term: Hit-to-Lead

Definition: The stage in drug discovery where screening hits undergo structural validation, analog synthesis, and initial SAR evaluation to select promising lead series.

Source: MED-001; MED-002

Status: Verified

## MED-T003

Term: Lead Optimization

Definition: The iterative synthetic modification of a lead chemical series to balance target potency, selectivity, ADME properties, and safety liabilities to yield a clinical candidate.

Source: MED-001; MED-005

Status: Verified

## MED-T004

Term: Pharmacophore

Definition: The ensemble of steric and electronic features (hydrogen bond donors/acceptors, aromatic rings, hydrophobic centers, charges) necessary to ensure optimal supramolecular interactions with a specific biological target.

Source: MED-001

Status: Verified

## MED-T005

Term: Half-Maximal Inhibitory Concentration (IC50)

Definition: The concentration of a drug or inhibitor required to inhibit 50% of a biological target's activity under specified in vitro assay conditions.

Source: MED-001; MED-005

Status: Verified

## MED-T006

Term: Structure–Activity Relationship (SAR)

Definition: The correlation between systematic modifications in chemical structure and resulting changes in biological activity or physical properties.

Source: MED-001; MED-005

Status: Verified

## MED-T007

Term: Ligand Efficiency (LE) & Lipophilic Efficiency (LLE)

Definition: Quantitative metrics normalizing target binding affinity relative to molecular size (LE = $Delta G^circ / N_{	ext{heavy}}$) or lipophilicity (LLE = $pIC_{50} - log P$).

Source: MED-005

Status: Verified

## MED-T008

Term: ADME

Definition: Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, and Excretion; the four fundamental pharmacokinetic processes dictating drug concentration at the target site over time.

Source: MED-001; MED-006

Status: Verified

## MED-T009

Term: Distribution Coefficient (Log D7.4)

Definition: The ratio of total equilibrium concentrations of all species (neutral and ionized) of a solute in octanol versus aqueous buffer at physiological pH 7.4.

Source: MED-001; MED-004

Status: Verified

## MED-T010

Term: Off-Target Liability (hERG / CYP)

Definition: Unwanted interaction with critical safety targets such as the hERG potassium channel (causing cardiac QT prolongation) or cytochrome P450 enzymes (causing drug-drug interactions).

Source: MED-004; MED-006

Status: Verified

## MED-T011

Term: Prodrug

Definition: A pharmacologically inactive compound converted in vivo by enzymatic or chemical transformation into the active drug molecule.

Source: MED-001

Status: Verified

## MED-T012

Term: Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API) & Polymorphism

Definition: The chemically defined therapeutic substance in a medicine; polymorphism refers to the ability of an API to crystallize in distinct solid-state crystal packings with differing solubility and stability.

Source: MED-004; MED-006

Status: Verified

## MED-T013

Term: Impurity Profiling (ICH Q3A/B)

Definition: The analytical identification, quantitation, and safety qualification of degradation products, synthesis byproducts, and residual solvents in API manufacture according to ICH limits ($> 0.10%$).

Source: MED-004; MED-006

Status: Verified

## MED-T014

Term: Bioisostere

Definition: Chemical substituents or groups possessing near-equal physical or chemical properties producing broadly similar biological properties in a therapeutic molecule.

Source: MED-001

Status: Verified
