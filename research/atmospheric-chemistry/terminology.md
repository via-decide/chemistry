# Atmospheric Chemistry — Terminology & Controlled Vocabulary

## ATM-T001

Term: Atmospheric Chemistry

Definition: The branch of atmospheric science investigating the chemical composition, reaction kinetics, photochemical transformations, and transport of trace gases and aerosols in Earth's atmosphere.

Source: ATM-001; ATM-002

Status: Verified

## ATM-T002

Term: Hydroxyl Radical (OH)

Definition: The primary daytime atmospheric oxidant ("atmospheric detergent"), driving the chemical degradation of most volatile organic compounds and pollutants.

Source: ATM-001; ATM-002

Status: Verified

## ATM-T003

Term: Photolysis Frequency (J-Value)

Definition: The first-order rate coefficient governing the solar photo-dissociation of a gas-phase molecule into radical or molecular products.

Source: ATM-001; ATM-003

Status: Verified

## ATM-T004

Term: Secondary Organic Aerosol (SOA)

Definition: Aerosol particles formed in the atmosphere from the condensation of low-volatility oxidation products of biogenic or anthropogenic volatile organic compounds.

Source: ATM-002

Status: Verified
