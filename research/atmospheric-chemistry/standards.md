- Title: Glossary of Atmospheric Chemistry Terms
- Publisher: IUPAC / Pure and Applied Chemistry
- Year: 1990
- DOI: 10.1351/pac199062112167
- Type: Terminology recommendation
- Reliability: High
- Evidence: The IUPAC document identifies itself as *Glossary of Atmospheric Chemistry Terms (Recommendations 1990)*, compiled and prepared for publication by Jack G. Calvert. [7](https://publications.iupac.org/pac/1990/pdf/6211x2167.pdf)
- Evidence: The synopsis states that over 620 atmospheric chemistry terms in common use by the international research community in atmospheric sciences are defined. [7](https://publications.iupac.org/pac/1990/pdf/6211x2167.pdf)
- Evidence: The synopsis states that the diverse backgrounds of scientists involved in atmospheric chemistry require attention to consistent terminology. [7](https://publications.iupac.org/pac/1990/pdf/6211x2167.pdf)
