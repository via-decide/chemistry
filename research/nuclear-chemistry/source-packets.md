# Nuclear Chemistry — Atlas Source Packets

These packets establish authoritative evidence coverage for the planned spreads. Packet verification does not promote a spread to final illustration or copy status.

## Packet NUC-SP-S043

Spread: S043, pages 85–86

Visual subject: Nuclide chart organized by proton number, neutron number, nuclear state, and evaluated decay properties.

Authoritative sources: NUC-002; NUC-003; NUC-008; NUC-009

Supported claims: NUC-C003; NUC-C004; NUC-C006; NUC-C007

Required visual distinctions: proton number versus neutron number; element identity versus mass number; ground state versus isomer; evaluated value versus missing value; stable or long-lived region versus decay-mode region; chart cell versus decay path.

Data requirement: Freeze a NuDat or ENSDF retrieval version, fields, filtering rules, transformation code, checksum, and license before rendering the heat map.

Known limitation: The verified sources establish the data system and chart interface, not a frozen atlas dataset or a universal stability-color taxonomy.

Packet status: Verified

Spread status: Needs Research

## Packet NUC-SP-S044

Spread: S044, pages 87–88

Visual subject: Decay constant, half-life, activity, population, elapsed time, and nuclear mass–energy balance.

Authoritative sources: NUC-004; NUC-005; NUC-006; NUC-007; NUC-010; NUC-011; NUC-012

Supported claims: NUC-C005; NUC-C013; NUC-C014; NUC-C015; NUC-C016; NUC-C017; NUC-C020

Equation records: NUC-EQ001; NUC-EQ002; NUC-EQ003; NUC-EQ004; NUC-EQ005

Required callouts: reference time; population `N`; decay constant `\lambda`; activity `A`; half-life `t_{1/2}`; exponential assumption; becquerel; mass convention; electron bookkeeping; sign of `Q`; energy and momentum partition.

Known limitation: Branching, decay chains, ingrowth, biological clearance, measurement uncertainty, and worked nuclide values remain outside the verified equation set.

## Packet NUC-SP-S045

Spread: S045, pages 89–90

Visual subject: Radiation source, geometry and shielding, HPGe detector, acquisition system, spectrum analysis, and measurement uncertainty boundaries.

Authoritative sources: NUC-005; NUC-013; NUC-014; NUC-018

Supported claims: NUC-C011; NUC-C012; NUC-C019; NUC-C021

Equation records: NUC-EQ006

Required callouts: source identity and geometry; source-to-detector distance; useful and unwanted radiation paths; shielding or collimation boundary; detector; high-voltage and acquisition electronics boundary; live time; energy and efficiency calibration; background; peak area; emission probability; correction factors; reported uncertainty.

Known limitation: The NIST procedure is a specific calibrated HPGe gamma-spectrometry workflow. It cannot represent gas, scintillation, neutron, dosimetry, or imaging systems without new sources.

Packet status: Verified

Spread status: Needs Research

## Packet NUC-SP-S046

Spread: S046, pages 91–92

Visual subject: Isotope production, chemical processing or separation, product quality, authorized application, controlled storage, return or recovery, and waste disposition.

Authoritative sources: NUC-015; NUC-016; NUC-017; NUC-018

Supported claims: NUC-C008; NUC-C009; NUC-C010; NUC-C018; NUC-C022

Required stages: reactor, accelerator, or generator route; irradiated target or precursor; chemical processing into required form; quality-control release where applicable; transport and authorized receipt boundary; application; inventory and security controls; reuse, return, recycling, conditioning, storage, or disposal branch.

Known limitation: Unsealed medical products and sealed industrial or medical sources have different controls and end-of-life routes. The final flow must branch by source form and jurisdiction and must not imply one universal process.
