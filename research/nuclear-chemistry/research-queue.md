# Nuclear Chemistry — Research Queue

## Primary and Classic Literature

- Acquire and verify the original Becquerel radioactivity reports.
- Acquire and verify the Curie radioactivity papers with exact editions, translations, and claim locators.
- Acquire and verify Rutherford and Soddy nuclear-transformation and decay-series papers.
- Acquire and verify Soddy's isotope papers and the first nuclide-chart publications.
- Add primary isotope-production, radiochemical-separation, detector-development, and tracer-application papers only when their page role is declared.

## Nuclide Data and Decay Models

- Freeze an evaluated database release or retrieval snapshot for the S043 chart and record its license, schema, checksum, and plotting transformation.
- Define stability, observational stability, ground state, isomer, branching ratio, and decay-region color rules before chart rendering.
- Add coupled Bateman equations, branching-decay models, parent–daughter ingrowth, activation, secular equilibrium, and uncertainty propagation.
- Add exact atomic-mass and decay-energy data records for every worked S044 example.

## Measurement and Safety

- Add a detector-neutral review covering gas-filled, scintillation, semiconductor, neutron, and imaging detectors.
- Add Poisson counting statistics, background subtraction, dead-time models, detection limits, calibration traceability, and uncertainty budgets.
- Add current IAEA safety requirements and jurisdiction-specific authorization, dose, contamination-control, transport, and emergency procedures.
- Verify shielding examples by radiation type, energy spectrum, geometry, material, buildup treatment, and design objective.

## Production, Application, and End of Life

- Split reactor, accelerator, and generator production routes and verify representative yields, target chemistry, separation trains, and quality attributes.
- Verify one medical, one industrial tracer, one environmental, and one safeguards or forensic case with domain-specific primary or standards sources.
- Distinguish unsealed radiopharmaceutical waste, sealed-source return, reuse, recycling, conditioning, interim storage, and disposal pathways.
- Add material-accountancy, chain-of-custody, transport-package, and national-registry requirements with current regulatory locators.

## Visual and Editorial Boundaries

- Do not reuse the S043 nuclide heat map as a decay-pathway visual.
- Do not draw the NIST HPGe procedure as a universal radiation detector.
- Do not merge medical product release with sealed-source end-of-life management into one unbranched process.
- Do not promote any packet to final art until example values, uncertainty, safety callouts, and source locators pass independent review.
