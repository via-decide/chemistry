# Biochemistry — Standards

## IUPAC-IUBMB White Book

- Title: Biochemical Nomenclature and Related Documents
- Common name: White Book
- Editor: C. Liébecq
- Publisher: Portland Press
- Year: 1992
- Type: Biochemical nomenclature compendium
- Reliability: High
- Evidence: IUPAC identifies the White Book as *Biochemical Nomenclature and Related Documents*, 2nd edition, edited by C. Liébecq and published by Portland Press in 1992. [1](https://iupac.org/what-we-do/books/whitebook/)
- Evidence: The Queen Mary IUPAC biochemical nomenclature page lists White Book contents including amino acids, peptides and proteins, enzymes, nucleotides, nucleic acids and protein synthesis, carbohydrates, and lipids. [2](https://iupac.qmul.ac.uk/bibliog/white.html)

## IUPAC-IUBMB biochemical nomenclature committees

- Source: IUBMB
- Type: Nomenclature governance source
- Reliability: High
- Evidence: IUBMB states that IUPAC and IUBMB established the IUPAC-IUBMB Joint Commission on Biochemical Nomenclature and the Nomenclature Committee of IUBMB. [3](https://iubmb.org/about/committees/biochemical-nomenclature-committees/)
- Evidence: IUBMB states that the purpose of these committees is to facilitate communication of biochemical information by encouraging scientists to use generally understood terminology. [3](https://iubmb.org/about/committees/biochemical-nomenclature-committees/)
