# Biochemistry — Terminology & Controlled Vocabulary

## BCH-T001

Term: Biochemistry

Definition: The branch of science exploring chemical processes, molecular structures, energy transformations, and information storage within living organisms.

Source: BCH-001; BCH-002

Status: Verified

## BCH-T002

Term: Protein Structure Hierarchy

Definition: The 4-level structural organization of proteins: Primary (amino acid sequence), Secondary ($alpha$-helix, $eta$-sheet), Tertiary (3D monomeric fold), and Quaternary (multi-subunit oligomeric assembly).

Source: BCH-001; BCH-006

Status: Verified

## BCH-T003

Term: Enzyme & Active Site

Definition: A biological macromolecule (protein or ribozyme) that catalyzes specific chemical reactions at a specialized 3D catalytic pocket termed the active site.

Source: BCH-001; BCH-003; BCH-004

Status: Verified

## BCH-T004

Term: Substrate & Product

Definition: The reactant molecule ($S$) specifically bound and transformed by an enzyme into a chemical product ($P$).

Source: BCH-004

Status: Verified

## BCH-T005

Term: Michaelis Constant (Km)

Definition: The substrate concentration at which reaction rate reaches half of maximum velocity ($v = 0.5 V_{\max}$), reflecting apparent enzyme-substrate affinity.

Source: BCH-005

Status: Verified

## BCH-T006

Term: Maximum Velocity (Vmax) & Turnover Number (kcat)

Definition: $V_{\max}$ is the limiting reaction rate at complete enzyme saturation; $k_{\text{cat}} = V_{\max} / [E]_T$ is the number of substrate molecules transformed per active site per second.

Source: BCH-004; BCH-005

Status: Verified

## BCH-T007

Term: Catalytic Efficiency

Definition: The second-order rate constant $k_{\text{cat}} / K_m$ measuring how efficiently an enzyme converts substrate to product at low substrate concentrations.

Source: BCH-004; BCH-005

Status: Verified

## BCH-T008

Term: Competitive Inhibition

Definition: Reversible enzyme inhibition where inhibitor $I$ competes directly with substrate $S$ for binding to free enzyme active site $E$, increasing apparent $K_m$ without altering $V_{\max}$.

Source: BCH-004; BCH-005

Status: Verified

## BCH-T009

Term: Non-Competitive & Uncompetitive Inhibition

Definition: Non-competitive inhibitors bind $E$ and $ES$ complexes equally, decreasing $V_{\max}$ without changing $K_m$; uncompetitive inhibitors bind only $ES$ complexes, decreasing both $V_{\max}$ and $K_m$.

Source: BCH-004; BCH-005

Status: Verified

## BCH-T010

Term: Allosteric Regulation

Definition: The modification of enzyme activity through effector binding at a regulatory site distinct from the active site, inducing conformational changes and cooperative kinetics.

Source: BCH-001; BCH-004

Status: Verified

## BCH-T011

Term: ATP Energetic Coupling

Definition: The exergonic hydrolysis of adenosine triphosphate ($	ext{ATP} 	o 	ext{ADP} + 	ext{P}_i$, $Delta G^circ = -30.5 	ext{ kJ/mol}$) coupled to endergonic synthetic reactions to drive non-spontaneous cellular processes.

Source: BCH-001; BCH-002

Status: Verified

## BCH-T012

Term: Coenzyme / Cofactor

Definition: Non-protein organic molecules (e.g., NAD$^+$, FAD, CoA) or inorganic ions (e.g., Mg$^{2+}$, Zn$^{2+}$, Fe$^{2+}$) essential for enzyme catalytic function.

Source: BCH-001; BCH-003

Status: Verified

## BCH-T013

Term: Metabolic Flux

Definition: The actual rate of substrate passage through a metabolic pathway, controlled by enzyme levels, allosteric regulation, and cellular substrate availability.

Source: BCH-001; BCH-002

Status: Verified

## BCH-T014

Term: EC Number

Definition: Enzyme Commission numerical classification system (EC 1 to EC 7) categorizing enzymes by catalyzed reaction class: 1 Oxidoreductases, 2 Transferases, 3 Hydrolases, 4 Lyases, 5 Isomerases, 6 Ligases, 7 Translocases.

Source: BCH-003

Status: Verified
