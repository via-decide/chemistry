# Biochemistry — Common Misconceptions

## BCH-M001

Misconception: The Michaelis constant $K_m$ is always identical to the thermodynamic substrate dissociation constant $K_d$.

Correction: $K_m = (k_{-1} + k_{\text{cat}}) / k_1$. $K_m$ equals dissociation constant $K_d = k_{-1}/k_1$ only when product formation rate is negligible compared to dissociation ($k_{\text{cat}} \ll k_{-1}$, Rapid Equilibrium assumption).

Evidence: BCH-001; BCH-004; BCH-005

Status: Verified

## BCH-M002

Misconception: Enzymes alter the equilibrium constant $K_{eq}$ or overall Gibbs free energy change $Delta G^circ$ of a biological reaction.

Correction: Enzymes accelerate forward and reverse reaction rates equally by lowering activation energy $Delta G^ddagger$. They do not change equilibrium concentrations or reaction thermodynamics.

Evidence: BCH-001; BCH-004

Status: Verified

## BCH-M003

Misconception: Competitive inhibitors destroy or permanently alter the target enzyme active site.

Correction: Competitive inhibition is reversible. At high substrate concentration ($[S] \to \infty$), substrate displaces the competitive inhibitor from the active site and reaction velocity reaches uninhibited $V_{\max}$.

Evidence: BCH-004; BCH-005

Status: Verified

## BCH-M004

Misconception: Static 2D metabolic chart maps represent constant, unvarying flow rates in living cells.

Correction: Metabolic flux is dynamic and heavily regulated by allosteric feedback, covalent modification (e.g., phosphorylation), enzyme transcription, and intracellular compartmentation.

Evidence: BCH-001; BCH-002; BCH-003

Status: Verified
